From my point of view, a good developer combines several qualities. The evolution of those qualities are not limited in time.

Step 1

Programming starts often with a blank page. There is no predefined path, a lot of possibilities stay opened. Creativity is of course not limited to the start stage, but is applied to the whole creation process. Facing a problem requires also creativity: there are often multiple solutions to a single problem.

Step 2

A strong logical mind is necessary. Good code requires a well defined structure and organisation. Logic is applied as much on whole sections (general, helicopter view) as on particular functions (quantic view). Logic defines a coherent story of instructions.

Step 3

According to me, optimisation is a central point in programming. Optimisation is closely tied with logic and creativity. Optimisation is the faculty to improve the efficiency of coding, finding better ways to organise code, reduce repetitions. Optimisation improves readability, maintenability, making the code user-friendly for everyone, including yourself.

Step 4

Determination is an important soft skill. It allows you to not giving up while encountering difficulties. Everyone who codes know that bugs are impossible to avoid: there is always something that occur that we did not or could not expect. And sometimes, a bug is not corrected at first try. Don't give up.

Step 5

Beeing curious is key to stay up-to-date. Technology evolves fast, practices too, something that is common nowadays can easely becomes obsolete in the near future. Therefore, a good way to maintain correct practices is to follow the evolution of your technology. Priority should be given to the official documentation.

The order of the points presented in this article does not matter.
